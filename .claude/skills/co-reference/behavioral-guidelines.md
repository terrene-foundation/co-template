# Behavioral Guidelines for Framework Discussions

This is a supporting reference for the `co-reference` skill. It governs how to respond when a conversation turns to the CARE / EATP / CO frameworks or to CO's proven domain application, COC. The `co-reference` SKILL.md carries the substance (8 principles, 5 layers, 6 phases, domain applications); this file carries the conduct.

## General Rules

- **Ground answers in the specs** — the Foundation-published specifications (CARE, EATP, CO) are the authoritative source. State what the spec says; flag clearly when you are extrapolating beyond it. CO is published under CC BY 4.0 by the Terrene Foundation.
- **Be honest about limitations** — every framework explicitly names what it does not solve. Quote the limitation rather than papering over it. The `co-reference` SKILL.md has an "Honest Limitations" section; use it.
- **Distinguish normative claims from ontological ones** — the Trust Plane / Execution Plane separation is a useful, deliberate framing of how human and machine roles divide, not a discovered law of nature. Present it as a design choice with reasons, not as fact.
- **Treat the trinity as a quartet with COC** — CARE (philosophy), EATP (protocol), and CO (methodology) are the trinity. They are peers, not competing alternatives — each answers a different question. When the discussion turns practical, add COC (CO for Codegen) as the fourth reference point: it is the proven domain application that demonstrates the trinity holds up in real practice. Use COC as evidence, not as a competitor to CO.

## CARE-Specific

- The Trust Plane is **permanently human** — this is a normative choice about where judgment belongs, not a temporary technical limitation that better models will erase.
- CARE's human competencies are described as **current AI limitations, not principled impossibilities** — be careful not to overstate them as permanent boundaries.
- CARE is **execution-tool-agnostic** — it sits above any AI execution tool and does not depend on a specific one.

## EATP-Specific

- Be precise with terminology — Genesis Record, Delegation Record, Constraint Envelope, Capability Attestation, and Audit Anchor each carry a specific meaning. Do not use them loosely or interchangeably.
- **Distinguish traceability from accountability** — this is EATP's most important distinction. Traceability is being able to reconstruct what happened; accountability is a human bearing responsibility for it. They are not the same, and conflating them is the most common error.
- **Connect EATP to CARE** — EATP operationalizes CARE's governance philosophy. When asked about EATP in isolation, surface that it exists to make CARE's trust boundaries enforceable.

## CO-Specific

- **Be domain-agnostic** — CO applies to any domain (research, governance, education, finance, codegen, and future domains). Use neutral language; do not let a single domain's vocabulary stand in for the methodology. When you need a concrete example, draw from more than one domain so the cross-domain reach stays visible.
- **Use the CO v1.2 vocabulary** — the six phases are Analyze, Plan, Execute, Review (canonical command `/vet`), Codify, Deliver. The eight principles and five layers are as listed in the `co-reference` SKILL.md. Do not fall back to earlier phase names (Phase 05 is Codify, not "Learn").
- **Reference COC as proof** — when someone doubts that CO works in practice, point to COC as the mature, proven domain application rather than arguing in the abstract.
- **Be honest about maturity** — CO was formalized as a domain-agnostic methodology after COC proved the pattern in one domain. The base methodology is younger than its first application; say so when maturity is relevant.

## COC-Specific

- **Ground in the three failure modes** — Amnesia, Convention Drift, and Safety Blindness are the root problems COC and CO exist to address. Frame COC's value in terms of which failure mode each mechanism counters.
- **Emphasize institutional knowledge** — the durable advantage is the organization's context and judgment, not raw model capability (CO Principle 1: AI capability is commodity; institutional knowledge is the differentiator). Keep returning to this when COC's value is questioned.
- **Treat COC's five layers as implemented, not theoretical** — COC demonstrates the five-layer architecture in working practice across agents, skills, rules, hooks, and commands. When citing COC, cite it as a working system, not a proposal.
